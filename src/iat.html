GAMEPLAY
Integrity of the platform's own rules and mechanics.
HARM

OPERATIONS

SOCIAL

The framework

Invariant-Anchored Telemetry (IAT)

A three-level derivation that grounds universal properties in observable telemetry — and binds named threats to the signals that detect them.

01
INVARIANTS
Universal properties

What must hold?

Tend to hold across platforms and time. Independent of features.

EXAMPLE
"Player identity must not be impersonable."
↓
02
TELEMETRY
Observable evidence

What do we measure?

Each invariant produces required telemetry — events, fields, and emission triggers tied to platform surface.

EXAMPLE
identity_create_or_rename { player_id, handle, similarity_to_existing, decision }
↓
03
THREATS
Detections bound to telemetry

What are we catching?

Named for the ecosystem. Illustrative, not exhaustive. Each threat binds to a filter on the telemetry above.

EXAMPLE
"Look-alike username" · "Homoglyphs" · "Impersonation account"

Threat categories shift across platforms. Telemetry varies with platform surface. Invariants stay fixed. IAT makes that boundary explicit — and traceable both ways.

G.H.O.S.T · The operationalization

Test-Driven Threat Modeling (TDTM)

Tests bind invariants to telemetry to threats. Walk the chain forward to design defenses. Walk it back to investigate incidents.
