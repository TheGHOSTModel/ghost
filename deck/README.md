- The five GHOST domains (G · H · O · S · T)
- The six-surface player model (Identity, Interaction, Shared World, Value, Creation, Operations)

- Hackathon or conference presentations

## Presentation notes

Detailed speaker notes are embedded in each slide. Key talking points per section:

**Opening:** Lead with the insight that most gaming threats need nothing broken — they use the platform as designed. The grooming attack, the coordinated load, the reputation manipulation — all legitimate interactions crossing domain boundaries undetected.

**The five domains:** Use the "society" framing — a multiplayer game is a living digital society with an economy, social structure, governance, and infrastructure. Each domain maps to a layer of that society.

**IAT:** Emphasise the three-level structure: invariant → telemetry → detection signal. The invariant is the property. The telemetry is the proof. The signal is the alert. Missing any one of the three means the platform is blind.

**AGE-X:** Walk through a worked example — pick H03 (minors protected from exploitation), score it across all seven dimensions, and show why it scores Critical on most platforms.
